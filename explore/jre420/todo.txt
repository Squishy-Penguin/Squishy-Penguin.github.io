<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Oftentimes, we experience </span><b>conflict and stress </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">when there is a </span><b>lack of agreement</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">. This can involve</span><b> hurt feelings</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">, </span><b>paranoia </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">(loss of trust), which can be handled through </span><b>pausing and doing a root-cause analysis</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> to find the trigger or the source of the problem.&nbsp;</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Conflict: </span><b>when one person/group subunit frustrates another&rsquo;s goal attainment</b></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">BATNA (best outcome)</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">WATNA (worst outcome)</span></li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Causes include:&nbsp;</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Ambiguity</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (role &amp; responsibility unclear, instructions unclear, goals unclear, etc.) often involves </span><b>lack of clarity &amp;missed assignments of the task</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">. This often unsettles people.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Interdependence (power imbalance)</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">, or</span><b> the organization&rsquo;s subculture segregates the people</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Group identification &amp; intergroup bias </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">(eg. team that received this month&rsquo;s bonus sees others as inferior) &rarr; superiority can be in terms of </span><b>time, money, headcount, resources, or talent</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></li>
</ul>

<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Power is the </span><b>capacity to influence others (often in a state of dependence)</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">. People who hold power are </span><b>relevant, visible, and extraordinary.</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> They can be from those who demonstrate care, and can be non-negative. They are the </span><b>most prominent when there is risk or a big change</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">. Power can be held by outsiders, sub-peers and superiors.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Note that: </span><b>resistance is less desirable than compliance, which is less desirable than commitment.&nbsp;</b></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>LEGITIMATE</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">: a formal kind of power, such as from a role on a job &rarr; leads to compliance.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>REWARD</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">: ability to provide or not provide outcomes ( eg. praise, compliment) &rarr; leads to compliance.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>COERCIVE</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">: punishment, threat&rarr; leads to resistance, and can be exerted by anyone.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>REFERENT</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">: well-liked (role model) &rarr; leads to commitment.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>EXPERT</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">: talent, special information/expertise is valued &rarr; leads to employee effectiveness and commitment.&nbsp;</span></li>
</ul>

<p><b>Equity </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">is for overcoming systematic advantages and disadvantages.&nbsp;</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Privilege</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Implicit discrimination. This can include exclusion and intentional targeting.</span><em><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Eg. &ldquo;It&rsquo;s great that you don&rsquo;t have an accent.&rdquo; Is it rude? But it&rsquo;s a compliment. However, it separates and points out the person. It is intentional and strategic. Ignores other aspects that makes them different and is relevant and instead focuses on a visible, non-relevant part of their identity.&nbsp;</span></em></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Identity</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Inclusion (sustainable, all people included)</span></li>
</ul>
<p><b>Equality</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> is a method used where everyone receives the same benefit/reduction.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">&ldquo;Is this ethical?&rdquo;&nbsp;</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>UTILITARIAN</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> -- does it benefit the greatest number of people (benefits majority). Weakness: mutual benefits and minorities ignored, benefits (especially long term ones) are hard to predict accurately</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>DEONTOLOGICAL (DUTY) </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">-- have people been treated with dignity and respect (everyone has a duty and must follow it). Weakness: principles and duties can clash, one can be right even with bad consequences. Strength: strongest for PR ethics.&nbsp;</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>VIRTUE (HR) </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">-- what makes a good PR (decision depends on featured witness). Weakness: conflict in virtues, can miss obligations to client and public.&nbsp;</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>DIVERSITY </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">--depends on the situation, every situation is unique. Weakness: risks biased judgement, complex and requires more resources and time to make an accurate decision.&nbsp;</span></li>
</ul>
<p><b>Ethics is not the same as morals</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (eg. eating a pizza by yourself is not ethically wrong, but can go against a person&rsquo;s morals).&nbsp;</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Socialization &amp; culture involves compliance, identification, patterns, and the organization&rsquo;s social traits. People learn these through a process that involves learning attitudes of the organization and then behaving to function in the organization.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><b>Social loafing</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> is a free rider and a sucker. Offering </span><b>performance visibility, interesting work, feedback, rewards</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">, and showing team members that they are </span><b>indispensable</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> can prevent social loafers from emerging.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">A </span><b>manager</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">&rsquo;s role is to deliver XYZ in a way that is best for an individual and the organization.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">A</span><b> leader</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">&rsquo;s role is much more long-term than a manager and looks at conflicts and opportunities for growth and change that exist.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">(I)</span><b>TRAIT APPROACH</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> - your leadership is predisposed by your personality. Weakness: absence of &ldquo;learn/change&rdquo; characteristics.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">(II)</span><b>TRANSACTIONAL</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> - leadership involves the attainment goals and objectives and is a longer trajectory than management. Leadership is building a platform to develop relationships with followers.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">(III)</span><b>TRANSFORMATIONAL </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">- leadership is having a better sense of performance and abilities of everyone. Leadership is supporting the need for personal growth and praise. Strength: provides motivation and drive.&nbsp; In order to be a fully transformational leader, all 4 must be present:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">IDEALIZED INFLUENCE - &ldquo;aspire to be them&rdquo;&nbsp;</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">INDIVIDUALIZED CONSOLIDATION - &ldquo;they came because of me&rdquo;</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">INSPIRATIONAL MOTIVATION - intrinsic motivation</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">INTELLECTUAL STIMULATION - challenge, independent thirst and creativity, nurturing</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">(IV) </span><b>VISION &amp; HEART </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">- leading with vision</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">(V) </span><b>AUTHENTIC</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> - action is consistent with</span><b> true values</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (internal moral perception).&nbsp;</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Fully self-aware</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Knows how to </span><b>balance strengths and weaknesses</b></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Transparency</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> in relationship</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Empowering</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">: team members feel good about the work they do, feel valued and impactful and part of the team in terms of psychology and structure. This is often influenced by the </span><b>resources</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> they are given and</span><b> highlights</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> of the employee&rsquo;s work.&nbsp;</span></li>
</ul>
<p><b>PATH-GOAL</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">: leader&rsquo;s job is to help a person attain work goals while achieving organization's goals in doing so. This involves determining the goals they want to achieve, making desired outcomes contingent on performance, clarifying path requirements and removing barriers that can impede performance, and </span><b>needs attributes and context in order to use appropriate behavior to guide them</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">. Weakness: </span><b>lacks a sense of fairness as a leader</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">(VI) </span><b>ETHICAL</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> - demonstrate openness, fairness, honesty. Involves personal actions and interpersonal relationships, and demonstrates &amp; promotes </span><b>integrity, altruism, empathy, personal growth, authenticity, empowerment, fairness and justice</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">(VII) </span><b>SERVANT </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">- no self-interest, is a group advocate. Involves empowerment, humility, authenticity, interpersonal acceptance, and provides direction and stewardship.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">(VIII) </span><b>UNETHICAL </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">- self-serving, deceptive. Involves lies, hypocrisy, arrogance, lack of social initiative, high self-monitoring, malicious, masked intentions.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">(IX) </span><b>PSEUDO-TRANSFORMATIONAL</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> - idealized influence however not well respected, apparent consideration (lasts as long as interaction), lacks care/empathy, for you to follow but not for your own good, discourages you to learn and develop.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">(X) </span><b>ABUSIVE </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">- cyclic, targets those that are weak and unable to defend themselves. Involves hostility, incivility, disrespect, coercion, and fear.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Within a group, there can be </span><b>satisficers and maximizers</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">. </span><b>Group think</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">, nominal group techniques, and the delphi technique are sometimes used. Tasks can be:</span></p>
<p><b>Additive: </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">performance proportional to group size, is a simple task therefore the more people the better.</span></p>
<p><b>Disjunctive:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> performance proportional to group size, is a task that is dependent on the performance of the best member (eg. research work, as long as there is a breakthrough it is a success).</span></p>
<p><b>Conjunctive:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> performance inversely proportional to group size, the team is dragged down by the worst-performing member (eg. assembly line).&nbsp;</span></p>
