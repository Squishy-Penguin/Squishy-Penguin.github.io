Exploring

What are the chances? Uncertainty follows travellers on their journey. 

An event can be composed of one (ie. elementary event) or multiple outcomes. Outcomes are results that cannot be decomposed into sub-events. A random experiment is where the outcome varies in an unpredictable fashion when repeated.. The set of all possible outcomes is the sample space. Sample spaces can be countable (ie. discrete) or continuous.

Axioms of Probability

  • 0 <= P[A]
    •  (probability of event A occurring is a non-negative value)
  • P[S] = 1
    •  (probability of event S, equivalent to the sample space, is one)
  • P[A’] = 1 - P[A]
    •  (probability of event A not occurring is the prime of probability of event A occurring)
  • A and B = null -> P[A or B] = P[A] + P[B]
    •  (if event A and B are mutually exclusive, then the probability of event A or event B occurring is the sum of the probability of event A, B)
  • A, B independent < = > P[A and B] = P[A]P[B]
    •  (events A, B are independent if and only if the probability of event A and B occurring is the product of probability of event A, B)
  • P [A|B] = P[A and B] / P [B]
    •  Conditional probability: probability of event A occurring given that event B has occurred is the product of the probability of event A and B occuring, divided by the probability of all instances where event B occurs
  • P [A|B] = P[B and A]P[A]/SUM( all P[B]’s)
    •  Baye’s Rule: probability of event A occurring given that event B has occurred is the product of the probability of event B and A occuring and probability of event A, divided by the probability of all instances where event B occurs
